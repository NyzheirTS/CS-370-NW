- What do computer scientists do and why does it matter?

Computer scientists explore solutions and design software systems that power everything from applications to AI infrastructure. They make sure everything runs smoothly by designing algorithms and data structures that are efficient, reliable, and scalable for millions of users.

---
- How do I approach a problem as a computer scientist?

As computer scientists we first define the problem to be solved, we then model it defining our inputs, outputs, and foreseeable constraints. Next we choose the appropriate language and stack to design our solution, we analyze the performance of the solution and test and refine the solution into its final form. 

---
- What are my ethical responsibilities to the end user and the organization?

Major ethical concerns are user privacy and safety, making sure to avoid harm and bias, and providing reliable avenues for users to address possible concerns. It is paramount that we build secure and reliable systems that serve its users responsibly.
